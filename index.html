02. Structural Engineering Logic

ISO Modifications & Core Ties

Standard ISO shipping containers rely on corrugated sidewalls to distribute shear and vertical forces. When combining two containers to construct spacious 1-Bedroom units (as visible in floorplan-TYP.jpg), structural cutouts are required. We reinforce the compromised structural envelope by placing physical sister beams and columns.

Modifications: Side Wall Removal

# STRUCTURAL CHECKLIST
Over-Head Column Insertion: HSS 150x150x6mm
Corner Casting Twist-Lock Locking Engagement
Mid-Span Bolted Flange Connector Alignment
Floor-Line Structural Sister-Joist Retrofit

The central demising seam features an overhead beam spanning the full unit length (clearly defined as "OVERHEAD BEAM" in the floorplan-TYP.jpg blueprint). This transfers live and static weight to corner structural members, securing stacking capabilities up to 4 levels.

Tension, Wind, and Seismic Locking

Stacked modular blocks must behave as a singular structure under dynamic wind loads and seismic events. Standard twist-locks at the corner castings must be coupled with heavy perimeter tie plate welding.

05. Pre-Fab & Assembly Workflows

Logistics & Erection Procedures

Modular volumetric construction succeeds when sequence planning is robust. This guide breaks down the off-site and on-site alignment procedures.

01

Factory Frame Preparation & Structural Modifications

Standard 40ft High-Cube steel container templates are modified. Side wall spans are cut according to layout designs, and heavy-duty structural steel headers are welded to transfer mechanical loads to corner castings.

02

Volumetric Framing, Rough-Ins & Core Shell Envelope

Framing is installed alongside complete mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP) rough-ins. In alignment with section A-A1.jpg, wet wall systems are pressure tested before closed-cell spray insulation is applied to prevent thermal bridging.

03

Ground-Floor Podium & Core Erection

Simultaneously, the on-site team pours foundations and constructs the ground floor podium and vertical circulation core. Heavy structural steel is placed to receive the incoming modular building blocks.

04

The "Flying Phase": Unit Craning & Envelope Closure

Using heavy-duty crawler cranes, individual units are lifted and placed into position. Twist-locks join corners securely, and site utility connections are made in the corridor spaces.
